Tirzepatide

ApprovedFDA-approved prescription medicine

A once-weekly, FDA-approved dual-action medication — the largest average weight loss of the approved options, around 21%.

✦ 2 min read · 2 sources

What it is

Tirzepatide activates two gut-hormone pathways (GIP and GLP-1) rather than one. It is FDA-approved as Zepbound for chronic weight management (and obstructive sleep apnea in adults with obesity) and as Mounjaro for type 2 diabetes — the same drug. Once-weekly injection.

How it works

Like semaglutide it activates GLP-1, but it also activates GIP, a second hormone involved in appetite, blood sugar, and fat handling. The combined effect curbs hunger, slows stomach emptying, and increases fullness — the two-pathway design is thought to be why it tends to outperform single-pathway GLP-1 drugs.

What the evidence shows

In SURMOUNT-1 (adults with obesity, no diabetes), tirzepatide averaged ~21% weight loss at 15 mg over 72 weeks vs ~3% placebo. In SURMOUNT-5, the first head-to-head, it led to ~20% vs ~14% for semaglutide. Stopping treatment leads to substantial regain.

Status & safety

Prescription medicine. Side effects are predominantly gastrointestinal and usually mild-to-moderate during dose escalation. Rare serious risks include pancreatitis, gallbladder problems, and the same rodent-based thyroid-tumor boxed warning. Requires medical evaluation and monitoring.
